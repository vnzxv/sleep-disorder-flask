About the Project

Understanding Sleep
Through Data

This uses the Sleep Health and Lifestyle Dataset to train a Gaussian Naive Bayes classifier capable of predicting whether a person has Insomnia, Sleep Apnea, or no sleep disorder.

The model analyzes 8 health and lifestyle features — including stress levels, BMI, physical activity, and heart rate — to generate a probabilistic classification with confidence scores for each possible outcome.

⚠️ For educational purposes only. Not a medical diagnostic tool.

No Disorder

58.6% of dataset. No significant sleep disorder detected. Normal sleep patterns based on lifestyle indicators.

Insomnia

20.6% of dataset. Difficulty initiating or maintaining sleep. Often linked to high stress and poor sleep quality.

Sleep Apnea

20.9% of dataset. Repeated breathing interruptions during sleep. Associated with higher BMI and elevated heart rate.

Eight health and lifestyle indicators feed the Naive Bayes classifier. Information Gain analysis found Stress Level, BMI, and Quality of Sleep to be the most predictive.

The Model

Gaussian Naive Bayes

A probabilistic classifier based on Bayes' Theorem. Assumes feature independence and models each feature with a Gaussian distribution.

Why Naive Bayes for this dataset?

With only 374 records and 8 mixed features, Naive Bayes is ideal — it trains instantly, handles small datasets gracefully, and outputs class probabilities rather than just a hard label.

The Gaussian variant is used because our numeric features (Age, Heart Rate, Sleep Duration, etc.) are assumed to follow a normal distribution within each class.

P(Class | Features) = P(Features | Class) × P(Class) / P(Features)
